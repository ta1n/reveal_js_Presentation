Zener Diode

What is a "Zener Diode" ?
  • a special type of diode
  • designed reliably allow current to flow "backwards"-
    • in a certain condition.
Other properties of zener diode -
  • Heavily doped
  • High power dissipation capability
  • Acts as a normal diode in the forward bias
  • Zener Breakdown Voltage is much lower than Avalanche Breakdown voltage
    (Usually, $V_{Avalanche Breakdown} > 6$ , $V_{Zener Breakdown}<6$)

Zener Voltage :The voltage that is sufficient for the reverse breakdown condition thus allowing the diode to conduct in the reverse direction.

Zener voltage is given by the manufacturer.

On state : When the voltage accross the zener diode is equal to or greater than Zener voltage

Off state : When the voltage accross the zener diode is less than Zener voltage

voltage in reverse bias doesn't change once it reaches $V_{Zener}$

Voltage Regulation

A Voltage Regulator is -
A system designed to automatically maintain a constant voltage.

So, in order to work as a voltage regulator, the zener diode will also have to maintain a constant voltage accross a load.

We learned - Once voltage accross the zener diode reaches "zener voltage", the voltage doesn't change.

So, if we take a zener diode, and connect it with a voltage source -

While ON state condition - it will maintain a constant voltage.

Let's connect the previous circuit with a load


  • We know, voltage accross parallel connected devices is the same,
  • So, voltage accross the diode and the load is the same.

  • On state condition : voltage accross zener diode = zener voltage
  • Finally we get, on state : Load voltage = zener voltage
So, we can now take a device that operates in that Zener voltage , and put it in parallel with the zener diode, and it will get stabilised voltage.

This is how we can use a zener diode as a voltage regulator
